# Narrative Foundations（敘事地基）

Three universal foundations of story production in this factory. They were
cut out line by line during the vinci_world world-entry rework (2026-07-06 —
2026-07-07, recorded in the umbrella repo's `STORY_REBUILD_PLAN.md` §二) and
apply to EVERY project — the per-game rulings that came out of the same
session live in that game's sovereignty files, not here.

Every module and step machine in this factory is downstream of these three.
The emotional beat sheet exists to hold #1, the delivery planner exists to
enforce #2, and the emotional-acceptance review line exists to test #3.

---

## 1. Emotion is the core; tokens come in two kinds

A story's upstream input is an emotional goal — what the player's chest
should do. Everything else is machinery. When writing or handing off, sort
every token you use into one of two kinds:

- **Tokens that TRANSMIT emotion**: saying them makes the listener's chest
  move before any explanation. Examples from the founding session:
  「媽媽大掃除把整盒卡丟了」(mom threw out the whole box during spring
  cleaning), 「零用錢只夠買一包，在店門口站了很久才拆」(allowance covered
  one pack; stood outside the shop a long time before opening it).
- **Tokens that merely CARRY emotion**: they need explaining before anyone
  feels anything. Examples: slab, 行會 (guild), pop report, 樂園 (the park).

The rule: creative work and handoffs set their direction with transmitting
tokens ONLY. Carrying tokens are demoted to furniture — they may appear in
the scenery, they never define what a beat is for. A beat sheet line written
in carrying tokens (「建立期望」"build anticipation") is an abstract
instruction, not a beat; rewrite it as a picture that transmits (「同船的人
聊自己想找什麼，有人的 want list 摺得又舊又軟」— fellow passengers talk
about what they hope to find; someone's want list is folded soft with age).

## 2. Place-first storytelling (the Disney A-side)

A story is LIVED first, TOLD second. Scenes, rituals, landmarks, and the
direction goods flow are all narrative channels; dialogue queues behind
them. The proof structure is the entry walk (car park → shuttle → gate →
tunnel → main street → castle at the street's end): all paperwork happens
OFF the path, and each section of the path does exactly one job — crossing
into another world.

Practical bindings:

- When a beat can be delivered by where things are, what people are doing,
  or which way the goods move, that delivery outranks a spoken line.
- An entry or transition路段 is a complete narrative structure of its own,
  not filler between two scenes. Give it beats.

## 3. Feel-consistency (the Disney C-side)

Every touchpoint delivers the SAME feel, unbroken across chapters and
scenes. This is an acceptance line, not an adjective: a review can FAIL an
artifact for breaking the feel even when canon, style, and form all pass.
The emotional-acceptance question in review gates operationalizes this:
which beat of the beat sheet did this output transmit, and did the curve's
holds and releases survive intact?
