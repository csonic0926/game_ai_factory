# STEP 2 — Identify Missing Roles and Excess Overlap

## Purpose

Identify what the current cast still lacks and where the current cast is too redundant.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/cast_management/CAST_SCOPE.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/cast_management/CAST_AUDIT.md`
- packaged characters under `<STORY_ROOT>/state/characters/`, when they exist

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/cast_management/CAST_MISSING_AND_OVERLAP.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Turn the saved cast scope and cast audit into a concrete missing-and-overlap diagnosis.

Use the scope and audit to identify the current cast's biggest gaps and redundancies in practical terms. Write the result so the next cast step can decide what to create, revise, or rebalance without re-reading the source files.

State the findings in a way that clearly names:

- the story functions the cast still needs
- the world-facing positions the cast still needs
- the pressure types the cast still needs or lacks in depth
- the characters or roles that are too similar in function, pressure, or voice
- the one or more cast moves that should happen first

## Required output blocks

### `MISSING ROLES`

List the roles, functions, positions, or pressure types the current cast still lacks.

For each item, include:

- the missing cast need
- the story reason it matters
- the kind of character or revision that would address it

### `OVERLAP TO REDUCE`

List the places where the current cast is too redundant.

For each item, include:

- the characters or role cluster involved
- the shared function, pressure, or voice shape
- the kind of overlap that should be reduced

### `PRIORITY ORDER`

State the current priority order for cast action.

List the next cast moves in order, and for each one include:

- what should happen first
- why it comes before the other moves
- whether it points toward creation, revision, or rebalance
