# STEP 3 — Institutions, Factions, and Daily Order

## Purpose

Define how power, obligation, and ordinary daily order are organized in the world.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_CONCEPT.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_RULES.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_GEOGRAPHY.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_INSTITUTIONS.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Turn the saved concept, rules, and geography into a concrete baseline for institutions, factions, professions, and daily order.

Write the result so a fresh sub-agent can identify:

- who organizes, governs, or controls ordinary life
- which aligned groups or factions matter in daily reality
- how ordinary work, service, trade, duty, or obligation is structured across a normal day
- what recurring pressures keep the social order functioning

## Required output blocks

### `INSTITUTIONS`

List the major organizing or power-holding bodies in short, direct entries.

For each institution, state:

- what it controls or organizes
- who depends on it
- what practical role it plays in ordinary life

### `FACTIONS`

List the major aligned groups that matter in this world in short, direct entries.

For each faction, state:

- what interest holds it together
- what distinguishes it from the others
- where its influence appears in daily life

### `DAILY ORDER`

State the recurring daily structure that ordinary people live within.

Show how work, duty, service, trade, movement, or supervision shapes a normal day.

### `COMMON PRESSURES`

List the routine demands, limits, or obligations that shape daily behavior in short, direct lines.

Each line should state:

- the pressure
- who feels it most
- how it shows up in ordinary life
