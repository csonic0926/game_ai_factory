# STEP 0.5 — World Concept Acceptance

## Purpose

Check whether the saved world concept is ready for later world-rules work.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_CONCEPT.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_CONCEPT_REVIEW.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Review the saved world concept against the acceptance criteria below and write a short pass/fail note.

## Acceptance criteria

### World premise

The concept passes when the premise states a clear core idea for the world and reads as an original world concept rather than a comparison-only pitch.

### Daily-life support

The concept passes when it explains how people normally live in this world and makes daily activity, obligation, or routine concrete enough to imagine in scene terms.

### Operating logic

The concept passes when it states what the world materially or socially runs on and gives enough operating logic to support later rules and institution work.

### Normal vs exceptional

The concept passes when it distinguishes ordinary actions from exceptional or disruptive ones and makes that distinction concrete enough to support later world constraints.

## Required stop condition

- Write a short note that says `STEP 0.5 PASS` or `STEP 0.5 FAIL`
- On `FAIL`, state the blocker clearly
