# STEP 2 — Geography and Locations

## Purpose

Define the world's spatial structure in a form that later institutions, movement logic, and digital-twin work can use directly.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_CONCEPT.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_RULES.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_GEOGRAPHY.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Turn the saved world concept and world rules into a concrete geography baseline.

Write the geography so later steps can use it to understand:

- what the major regions or settlement zones are
- where people actually live, work, gather, and pass through
- how locations relate to each other in space
- how ordinary movement happens between those locations

## Required output blocks

### `GEOGRAPHY OVERVIEW`

State the world's major spatial layers in clear, direct terms.

### `SETTLEMENT STRUCTURE`

State how settlement is organized.

Include the major inhabited zones, districts, quarters, towns, or other population structures that matter for daily life.

### `LOCATION GROUPS`

List the major location clusters that later steps are likely to use.

For each location group, state:

- what kind of place it is
- what people normally do there
- how it relates to nearby places

### `MOVEMENT PATHS`

State how people normally move between the major location groups.

Describe the ordinary routes, thresholds, bottlenecks, or access patterns that shape daily movement.
