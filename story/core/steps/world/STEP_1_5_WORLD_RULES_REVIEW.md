# STEP 1.5 — World Rules Review

## Purpose

Check whether the saved world rules are ready for later geography, institution, and daily-order work.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_RULES.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_CONCEPT.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_RULES_REVIEW.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Review the saved world rules against the acceptance criteria below and write a short pass/fail note.

## Acceptance criteria

### Core rules

The rules pass when they state how the world actually operates, are concrete enough to support later scene and world-design decisions, and read as world logic rather than abstract flavor.

### Practical constraints

The rules pass when ordinary people, groups, or institutions have explicit limits, those limits have practical effects that can be imagined in use, and the constraints help define what people can and cannot normally do.

### Consequence logic

The rules pass when they show how the world reacts when people push against them, likely consequences can be predicted from the written rules, and later steps would not need to guess how friction or enforcement works.

### Known exceptions

The rules pass when exceptions are named clearly, fit the world's rule system instead of replacing it, and do not swallow ordinary world logic.

## Required stop condition

- Write a short note that says `STEP 1.5 PASS` or `STEP 1.5 FAIL`
- On `FAIL`, state the blocker clearly
