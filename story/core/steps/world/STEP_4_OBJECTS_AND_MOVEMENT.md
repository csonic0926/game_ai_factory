# STEP 4 — Objects and Movement

## Purpose

Build the logistics baseline for ordinary objects, services, and movement in this world.

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_GEOGRAPHY.md`
- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_INSTITUTIONS.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/world_baselines/WORLD_LOGISTICS.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Use the saved geography and institutions baseline to write a concrete logistics baseline.

Produce a result that makes these facts easy to read:

- what common objects or goods appear in ordinary world use
- how services are requested, delivered, paid for, or routed
- how people, goods, messages, or authority normally move through the world
- where transfer, delay, handoff, or bottleneck pressure naturally appears

## Required output blocks

### `COMMON OBJECTS`

List the reusable objects, supplies, goods, tools, or documents that appear in ordinary world use.

For each object group, write:

- what the object group is
- what it is used for
- where it normally appears

### `SERVICE FLOWS`

List the common services that move through the world.

For each service flow, write:

- who provides it
- who uses it
- how it is requested, delivered, or paid for

### `MOVEMENT LOGIC`

State how people, goods, messages, or authority normally circulate.

For each movement pattern, write:

- who moves
- why they move
- what ordinary constraint, route, or limit shapes the movement

### `TRANSFER POINTS`

List the places, counters, gates, checkpoints, crossings, hubs, or handoff sites where something changes hands.

For each transfer point, write:

- what changes hands there
- why the point exists
- why the point matters for delay, control, or bottleneck pressure
