# STEP 2 — Story-Line Discovery

## Purpose

- choose the one ordinary day-track that should carry this chapter-day
- identify the concrete bend that turns that day-track into the chapter

## Read inputs from

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/chapter_sources/<ARTIFACT_STEM>_PREFLIGHT.md`

## Save output to

- `<STORY_ROOT>/state/chapter_sources/<ARTIFACT_STEM>_STORY_LINE.md`

## Skill use

- No skill required for this step.

## Task

Read the preflight and select one story line that should become the chapter-day.

Your result must state:

- what the chapter is about
- what ordinary day-track it starts from
- what concrete thing bends that day-track
- why this line is the best chapter line from the available preflight material

Choose a line that:

- starts from a believable ordinary day-track
- can carry pressure through the day
- can lead to visible movement and consequence
- stays grounded in the preflight material
- says something important about the current chapter state

Useful source material includes:

- obligation
- relationship
- object
- location-bound responsibility
- routine
- institution
- pressure pattern
- knowledge boundary

## Required output blocks

- `DISCOVERY QUESTION`
- `STORY LINE CANDIDATES`
- `SELECTED STORY LINE`
- `NORMAL DAY TRACK`
- `BENDING POINT`
- `WHY THIS MUST BE THE CHAPTER`

## Block definitions

### `DISCOVERY QUESTION`

State the chapter question this step answered.

### `STORY LINE CANDIDATES`

List 2-4 plausible lines from the preflight.
For each candidate, state:

- what the line is
- what ordinary day-track it begins from
- what kind of bend it creates

### `SELECTED STORY LINE`

State one chosen line only.

### `NORMAL DAY TRACK`

State what the day would have looked like if this line stayed ordinary.

Include:

- what the player thought today was going to be about
- what they were supposed to finish, return to, deliver, check, avoid, or handle

### `BENDING POINT`

State the concrete thing that makes the ordinary day stop staying ordinary.

Use a specific source of the bend, such as:

- person
- action
- interruption
- object
- demand
- discovery
- refusal
- delay
- mismatch between expectation and reality

### `WHY THIS MUST BE THE CHAPTER`

State why this line should become the chapter.

Explain:

- why this bend matters now
- why it is strong enough to shape the day
- why this is the line the chapter should follow
